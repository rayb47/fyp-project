Architecture in Portugal

Portugal is renowned for its rich architecural heritage, spanning across several centuries and a variety of styles, from Romanesque to Manueline, and from Baroque to Modern. Information on the six most famous architectural sites in Portugal are displayed below.

Interact with the maps and switch to Street View to get a better view of the sites.
Tower of Belém (Torre de Belém)

Located in the heart of Lisbon, this tower is a masterpiece of the Manueline style, combining elements of Gothic and Renaissance architecture. It was built in the early 16th century as a part of the defenses at the mouth of the Lisbon Harbor. Now, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most photographed landmarks of Portugal.

From Google maps by Antonio Alba
Jerónimos Monastery (Mosteiro dos Jerónimos)

Also located in Belem, Lisbon, the Jeronimos Monastery is one of the best examples of the late Gothic Architecture. It was built in the early 16th century by King Manuel I to commemorate the return of Vasco De Gama from his journey to India, and give thanks to God for the wealth it brought to Portugal. This site is also a part of the same UNESCO World Heritage designation as the Tower Of Belem.

From Google maps by Stefano Andrighetto
Pena Palace (Palácio da Pena)

Situated upon a hilltop in the Sintra mountains surrounded by lush forests and gardens, this palace is one of the finest examples of 19th century romanticism in the world. Today, the palace is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and considered to be one of the Seven Wonders of Portugal.

Dates: April 23 - May 30

From Google maps by Marunak F
Batalha Monastery (Mosteiro da Batalha)

Located in Batalha, this monastery is one of the most important Gothic sites in Portugal, renowned for its intricate Manueline flourishes. It was built to commemorate the Battle of Aljubarotta (1385) and took over a century to complete. It features a breathtaking array of sculptures, filigree and the famed Unfinished Chapels.

From Google maps by Cristian
Casa da Música

Located in Porto, Casa de Musica is one of the most strikingly modern and innovative architectural landmarks in Portugal. It was designed by Dutch Artist Rem Koolhas in 2001, and the construction was completed in 2005. It stands out not only for its distinct contemporary design, but also for its cultural significance as a principal venue for music and performing arts.

From Google maps by Tygo Klasse
Alcobaça Monastery (Mosteiro de Alcobaça)

The Alcobaça Monastery is one of the oldest and most impressive exapmples of Cistercian architecure in Portugal. Founded in the 12th century, the monastery is celebrated for the purity and clarity of its architectural style, and is particularly noted for the ornate tombs of King Pedro I and Inês de Castro.
